This is a *Dark Mode Cyber-Dashboard* aesthetic, specifically leaning into *Skeuomorphic Data Visualization* and *Glassmorphism*. It’s high-fidelity, cinematic, and prioritizes "vibe" over pure utility—perfect for an Antigravity project that needs to look high-tech and immersive.

Here is the breakdown of the style components you need to replicate:

### 1. Color Palette & Lighting

* *Deep Charcoal Base:* The background isn't pure black (); it’s a very dark, matte grey ( range).
* *Luminescent Accents:* Use high-saturation neon greens and teals for the "active" data. Notice the *Glow/Bloom effect*; the data points aren't just colored pixels, they emit a soft light that bleeds into the dark background.
* *Alert Contrast:* Warm oranges and muted reds are used sparingly for warnings, creating an immediate visual hierarchy against the cool-toned primary colors.

### 2. Typography & Layout

* *Technical Sans-Serif:* Use a clean, geometric sans-serif (think Inter, Roboto Mono, or Archivo). Keep tracking (letter spacing) tight for headings and wider for small technical data.
* *Information Density:* The layout is "modular." Everything is contained in rounded-corner cards or floating panes, but the borders are often invisible or defined only by a subtle glass blur.
* *Micro-Copy:* Notice the tiny "metadata" text (e.g., "Data based on 29/12 analysis"). This adds to the "pro-tool" aesthetic.

### 3. Data Visualization (The "Hero" Element)

* *Point Cloud Mapping:* The central map isn't a solid vector; it's a *topographic point cloud*. Replicate this using thousands of tiny dots or particles with varying opacity to create depth and texture.
* *Organic Flow:* The data looks fluid, almost like a heat map or bioluminescent plankton. Avoid rigid grids for your primary visuals; use organic, flowing shapes.
* *Abstract Indicators:* Icons are minimal—shields for protected areas, hazard triangles for threats. They are flat but wrapped in a subtle circular "halo."

### 4. UI Textures & Effects

* *Glassmorphism:* Use "Frosted Glass" (Backdrop Blur) for overlays and buttons. It adds layers of depth without cluttering the screen.
* *Subtle Gradients:* There are very faint radial gradients in the background to prevent the dark areas from looking "dead."
